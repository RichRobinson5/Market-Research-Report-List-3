<p><p>HVDC (High-Voltage Direct Current) Converter Transformers are crucial components in power transmission systems that convert electrical energy from AC to DC and vice versa. There are two types of HVDC converter transformers: Line Commutated Converter (LCC) and Voltage Source Converter (VSC). LCC converters operate using thyristor valves, while VSC converters utilize insulated gate bipolar transistors. LCC technology has higher efficiency for long-distance power transmission, while VSC offers better control for grid stabilization and interconnected AC systems. Both types play key roles in facilitating efficient and reliable power transmission over long distances.</p></p>
