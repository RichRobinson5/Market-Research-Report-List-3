<p><p>Deep sea mining refers to the extraction of valuable minerals from the ocean floor. The market for deep sea mining can be categorized into three types. Cobalt Rich Crusts (CRC) are formations rich in cobalt found on the seafloor, primarily in the Pacific Ocean. Manganese nodules are sedimentary deposits containing valuable metals like manganese, copper, and nickel, found in various ocean basins. Seafloor Massive Sulphides (SMS) are rich in minerals like copper, gold, and silver, formed around hydrothermal vents. These different types offer potential opportunities for the mining industry in the deep sea.</p></p>
