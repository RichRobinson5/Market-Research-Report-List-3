<p><p>A floating crane is a specialized type of crane that is mounted on a pontoon or a barge. It is commonly used in marine construction and port operations to lift heavy loads and handle various materials, such as containers, machinery, and bulk goods. Floating cranes are highly advantageous in situations where land-based cranes are not feasible or when a large outreach is required.</p><p>The floating crane market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. The increasing demand for floating cranes can be attributed to the rising investments in port infrastructure development and the expanding global seaborne trade. With the growth in cargo transportation through seaports, the need for efficient and versatile lifting equipment like floating cranes is also increasing.</p><p>Additionally, the growth of the offshore wind industry and oil and gas exploration activities has further stimulated the demand for floating cranes. These cranes play a crucial role in the installation and maintenance of offshore wind turbines, as well as in the positioning of drilling rigs and other equipment in the oil and gas sector.</p><p>Furthermore, technological advancements are also driving the market growth. Secondly, catamarans are floating cranes constructed with two parallel hulls, enabling high stability and efficient load handling. Thirdly, semi-submersibles are designed with partially submerged hulls, enhancing stability and weight-bearing capacity.
