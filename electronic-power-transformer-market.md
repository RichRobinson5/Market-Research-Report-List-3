<p><ul><li>Single-stage</li><li>Two-stage</li><li>Three-stage</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Electronic power transformers are categorized based on the number of stages in their design. Single-stage transformers are the simplest and most common type, converting voltage from one level to another in a single step. Two-stage transformers use two intermediary levels of voltage to achieve a more precise conversion. Three-stage transformers are the most complex, with three intermediary voltage levels for even more precise and tailored power conversion. Each type offers different levels of efficiency and precision in voltage conversion for various industrial and commercial applications.</p></p>
