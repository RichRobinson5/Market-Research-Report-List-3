<p><p>A thermoluminescence dosimeter (TLD) is a device that measures ionizing radiation doses by collecting and analyzing the light emitted from crystalline materials following exposure to such radiation. The TLD consists of a small chip made of lithium fluoride or other radiation-sensitive crystals, which emits light when exposed to radiation. This light emission is proportional to the amount of radiation absorbed by the crystal and is measured using a photomultiplier tube or a solid-state detector.</p><p>The thermoluminescence dosimeter market is expected to grow at a significant rate during the forecast period. The increasing applications of TLD in various industries such as healthcare, nuclear power plants, and radiology are driving the market growth. The TLDs are widely used in radiation therapy, where precise measurements of radiation doses are crucial for patient safety. The increasing adoption of radiation therapy for cancer treatment is further driving the demand for TLDs.</p><p>Additionally, the growing concerns regarding radiation safety and the need for accurate measurement of radiation doses for occupational exposure monitoring are fueling the market growth. The TLDs provide a reliable and cost-effective solution for radiation monitoring in such applications.</p><p>Moreover, technological advancements in TLDs, such as the development of compact and portable devices, are contributing to market growth.
