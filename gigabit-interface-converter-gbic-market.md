<p><p>Gigabit Interface Converter (GBIC) is a type of transceiver module used in networking to convert electrical signals into optical signals. Singlemode GBICs are designed for long-distance communication over single-mode fiber optic cables, while multimode GBICs are suited for shorter distances over multimode fiber optic cables. Singlemode GBICs are typically used in telecom and data center applications that require high bandwidth over long distances, while multimode GBICs are commonly used in enterprise networks and short-distance connections within buildings or campuses.</p></p>
