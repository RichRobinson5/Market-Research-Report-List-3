<p><p>Total Knee Arthroplasty (TKA) is a surgical procedure used to treat knee pain and restore joint function. The TKA market can be categorized into two types: fixed-bearing implants and mobile-bearing implants. Fixed-bearing implants involve attaching a metal or plastic implant to the thigh and shin bones, while the articular surface is fixed to the tibia component. In mobile-bearing implants, the articular surface is designed to allow slight movement, which may result in increased joint stability and improved long-term outcomes.</p></p>
