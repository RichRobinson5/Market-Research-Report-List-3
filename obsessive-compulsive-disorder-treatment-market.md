<p><p>Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD) is a mental health disorder characterized by repetitive and intrusive thoughts (obsessions) that lead to repetitive behaviors (compulsions) aimed at reducing anxiety. Treatment for OCD typically involves a combination of medication, therapy, and self-help strategies.</p><p>Medication options for OCD include selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs) such as fluoxetine, fluvoxamine, and sertraline, which help to regulate serotonin levels in the brain. These medications are often prescribed alongside therapy to enhance the treatment effects.</p><p>Therapy options for OCD include cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT), which helps individuals identify and change their negative thought patterns and behaviors. Exposure and response prevention (ERP) is a specific form of CBT that involves exposing individuals to their obsessions and preventing the associated compulsions. This helps them gradually learn to tolerate and reduce their anxiety.</p><p>The market for Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder treatment is expected to witness significant growth in the forecast period. The increasing prevalence of OCD and the rising awareness about mental health disorders are the primary factors driving market growth.
