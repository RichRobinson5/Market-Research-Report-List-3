<p><p>Industrial Computed Tomography (CT) machines are used for non-destructive testing and inspection of objects or materials. There are two types of CT scanning technologies used in the market. </p><p>1. Line Beam Scanning: This method utilizes a thin line of x-rays that scans across the object, capturing multiple images from different angles. These images are then reconstructed to create a 3D model, allowing for detailed analysis and measurement of internal structures.</p><p>2. Cone Beam Scanning: In this technique, a cone-shaped x-ray beam is used to capture a series of images as the object rotates. These images are stitched together to create a 3D model, providing comprehensive visualization of the object's internal features.</p><p>Both technologies have their specific applications and benefits, catering to various industrial sectors like automotive, aerospace, and electronics for quality control and defect detection purposes.</p></p>
