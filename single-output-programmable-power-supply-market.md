<p><p>The single-output programmable power supply market can be categorized into two types: AC and DC. AC programmable power supplies are used when alternating current is required, usually for devices that operate on AC voltage. They offer precise control over voltage, frequency, and phase, making them suitable for testing purposes. On the other hand, DC programmable power supplies are designed for devices that require direct current. They provide adjustable DC voltage and current, enabling efficient power delivery to various electronic components. Both AC and DC programmable power supplies serve specific applications within the electronics industry.</p></p>
