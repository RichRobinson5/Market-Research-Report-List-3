<p><p>A dashboard camera, also known as a dashcam or car DVR, is a device that captures video footage from the perspective of the driver's seat. Additionally, some dashcams also include features such as GPS tracking, motion detection, and parking mode.</p><p>The future of the dashboard camera market looks promising, as it is expected to grow at a CAGR of 10.6% during the forecasted period. One of the key drivers of this growth is the increasing number of road accidents and the need for concrete evidence for insurance claims and legal purposes. AI-powered dashcams can analyze road conditions, detect pedestrians and other vehicles, and provide real-time alerts to drivers, enhancing safety on the roads.</p><p>In terms of market trends, there is a growing preference for dual-channel dashcams that can record both the front and rear views of a vehicle simultaneously. This is particularly useful for capturing rear-end collisions or incidents during parking. Moreover, there is an increasing demand for dashcams with higher resolutions, such as 4K and 8K, to capture clearer and more detailed video footage.</p><p>To sum up, the dashboard camera market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years due to the increasing need for evidence in road accidents, the integration of dashcams with ADAS, and the adoption of AI and machine learning technologies. These dashcams offer more comprehensive surveillance and are commonly used by commercial fleet operators or for advanced security purposes.</p></p>
