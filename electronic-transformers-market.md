<p><p>Electronic transformers are devices used to transform electrical energy from one voltage level to another. They can be classified into two main types: low-voltage and high-voltage electronic transformers. </p><p>Low-voltage electronic transformers are designed to convert a low input voltage into a higher output voltage. They are commonly used in various electronic devices such as computers, televisions, and audio systems. These transformers are compact, efficient, and provide a stable power supply for low-voltage applications.</p><p>High-voltage electronic transformers, on the other hand, are primarily used in industrial and utility applications to step up or step down high voltages. They are capable of handling large power loads and ensure efficient transmission and distribution of electrical energy. These transformers are essential for powering heavy machinery, substations, and power transmission lines.</p></p>
