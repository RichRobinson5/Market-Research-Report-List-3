<p><p>Transdermal drug delivery system involves delivering drugs through the skin into the bloodstream, bypassing the gastrointestinal tract. This method is commonly used for pain management, hormone replacement therapy, and nicotine cessation. On the other hand, transmucosal drug delivery system involves delivering drugs through mucous membranes, such as the mouth, nasal passages, or rectum. This method allows for rapid drug absorption and is often used for quick pain relief or in emergency situations. Both systems offer advantages such as improved patient compliance and reduced side effects.</p></p>
