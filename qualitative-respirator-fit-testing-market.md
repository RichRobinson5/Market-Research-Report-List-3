<p><p>Qualitative respirator fit testing is used to determine if a respiratory mask effectively seals against the wearer's face. The market offers different types of fit testing methods. The Saccharin or Bitrex test involves the wearer wearing a mask and inhaling a spray containing a sweet-tasting solution. If the wearer can taste it, the mask is not sealing properly. Isoamyl Acetate test uses a pungent odor instead of taste. The irritant smoke test involves the wearer wearing a mask while an irritant smoke is released to check for leakage.</p></p>
