<p><p>Electrical Variable Optical Attenuators (EVOA) are devices used in optical networks to control the power of light signals. Single-channel EVOAs are designed to attenuate a single wavelength, providing precise control over the power level. They are commonly used in applications where individual channel control is required. On the other hand, 4-channel EVOAs can simultaneously attenuate four different wavelengths. These EVOAs enable efficient multi-wavelength control and can be used in complex optical communication networks that require adjustment of multiple channels. Both types play a crucial role in managing and optimizing optical power in various industries.</p></p>
