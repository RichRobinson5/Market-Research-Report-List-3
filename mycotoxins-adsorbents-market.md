<p><p>Mycotoxin adsorbents are substances used to bind and remove mycotoxins, which are toxic compounds produced by fungi. There are several types of mycotoxin adsorbents available in the market. </p><p>Hydrated aluminosilicates are natural minerals that have a high affinity for mycotoxins, effectively trapping and removing them from feed or food products. Bentonite is a type of hydrated aluminosilicate clay that is commonly used as a mycotoxin adsorbent.</p><p>Yeast cell wall extracts are derived from the cell walls of yeast and have the ability to bind mycotoxins, preventing their absorption in the digestive system.</p><p>Enzyme detoxifiers are specific enzymes that break down mycotoxins into non-toxic compounds, rendering them harmless.</p><p>Other mycotoxin adsorbents may include activated charcoal, certain activated clays, and modified non-digestible carbohydrates. These adsorbents work by either binding to mycotoxins or promoting their enzymatic degradation, ultimately reducing their toxicity.</p></p>
