<p><p>The water quality sensor market encompasses various types of sensors used to measure different parameters. The chlorine residual sensor measures the amount of chlorine in water, indicating its disinfection level. The TOC sensor measures the total organic carbon content, identifying possible contaminants. The turbidity sensor assesses water clarity by measuring suspended particles. The conductivity sensor measures the ability of water to conduct electrical current, indicating its purity. The pH sensor measures the acidity or alkalinity of water. Lastly, the ORP sensor measures the oxidation-reduction potential, assessing its ability to undergo chemical reactions. These sensors help ensure the safety and quality of water in various settings.</p></p>
