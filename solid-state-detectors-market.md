<p><p>Solid-state detectors are a type of radiation detectors that do not require a gas-filled chamber for operation. Semiconductor detectors are a type of solid-state detector that use semiconducting materials such as silicon or germanium to detect radiation. These detectors offer high resolution and sensitivity, making them ideal for applications such as medical imaging and security screening. Diamond detectors, on the other hand, use diamond as the detecting material, offering advantages such as high radiation hardness and temperature tolerance. Both types of detectors are widely used in various industries for radiation detection purposes.</p></p>
