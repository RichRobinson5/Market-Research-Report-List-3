<p><p>Interactive projectors are advanced devices used for presentations and collaborations. They come in three main types: DLP (Digital Light Processing), LCD (Liquid Crystal Display), and LCoS (Liquid Crystal on Silicon). DLP projectors use an array of micro mirrors to reflect light, resulting in high contrast and sharp image quality. LCD projectors consist of liquid crystals that manipulate light to display images. LCoS projectors combine the best qualities of DLP and LCD, using liquid crystals on a silicon chip to produce vibrant and detailed images. These different technologies offer users options to choose from based on their specific needs and preferences.</p></p>
