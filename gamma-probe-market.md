<p><p>A gamma probe, also known as a gamma detector or gamma counter, is a medical device used for intraoperative detection of radioactive substances in certain cancer-related procedures. It consists of a handheld probe that detects and measures the presence of gamma radiation emitted by radiopharmaceuticals administered to patients. The probe helps surgeons precisely locate and remove cancerous tissues or lymph nodes without damaging surrounding healthy organs or tissues.</p><p>The global gamma probe market has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is projected to continue growing at a CAGR of 9.60% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to several factors. Firstly, the increasing prevalence of cancer worldwide has led to a rise in cancer-related surgeries, driving the demand for gamma probes. Secondly, advancements in imaging technology and surgical techniques have increased the adoption of gamma probes in precision surgeries, further fueling market growth.</p><p>Moreover, the growing awareness about the benefits of early cancer detection and improved healthcare infrastructure in developing regions are contributing to the market expansion. Additionally, the development of more portable and user-friendly gamma probes has facilitated their use in ambulatory and rural healthcare settings, expanding their market reach.</p><p>One of the emerging trends in the gamma probe market is the integration of gamma probes with other imaging modalities, such as positron emission tomography (PET) and single-photon emission computed tomography (SPECT). This integration allows for more accurate and comprehensive imaging, enhancing surgical outcomes.</p><p>In conclusion, the global gamma probe market is experiencing steady growth due to the increasing prevalence of cancer, advancements in surgical techniques, and the growing adoption of precision surgeries. With ongoing technological advancements and the integration of gamma probes with other imaging modalities, the market is expected to witness further growth in the coming years.</p></p>
