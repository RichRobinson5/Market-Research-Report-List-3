<p><strong>The Automotive EMC Testing Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Bulk Current Injection (BCI)</li><li>Conducted Emissions (CE)</li><li>Conducted Transient Emissions (CTE)</li><li>Conducted Transient Immunity (CTI)</li><li>Electrostatic Discharge (ESD)</li><li>Radiated Emissions (RE)</li><li>Radiated Immunity (RI)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Automotive EMC Testing involves various types of tests to ensure electromagnetic compatibility of vehicles and their components. Bulk Current Injection (BCI) tests the immunity of components to high current surges. Conducted Emissions (CE) and Conducted Transient Emissions (CTE) assess the level of electromagnetic emissions from the vehicle. Conducted Transient Immunity (CTI) evaluates the vehicle's ability to withstand transient disturbances. Electrostatic Discharge (ESD) tests the vehicle's resistance to static electricity. Radiated Emissions (RE) and Radiated Immunity (RI) measure the vehicle's emissions and susceptibility to external electromagnetic fields.</p></p>
