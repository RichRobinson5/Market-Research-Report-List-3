Quadruple-junction GaAs Solar Cells have four semiconductor layers, allowing even higher efficiency due to their ability to capture a broader range of light. Single-junction GaAs Solar Cells use only one layer of semiconductor material, making them more affordable but with lower efficiency than the other two types.</p></p>
