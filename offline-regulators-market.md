<p><ul><li>Current Mode</li><li>Voltage Mode</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Offline regulators are divided into two main types: current mode and voltage mode. Current mode regulators control the output by adjusting the current flowing through the load, providing better transient response and stability. Voltage mode regulators, on the other hand, regulate the output by adjusting the voltage at the output, offering simpler control and design. Both types have their own advantages and are used in various applications depending on the specific requirements of the system.</p></p>
