<p><p>The surgical bed market consists of two main types: motorized and non-motorized beds. Motorized beds are equipped with electric controls that allow for easy adjustment of the bed's height, position, and inclination. These beds are ideal for surgical procedures as they provide efficient and precise patient positioning. On the other hand, non-motorized beds are manually operated and do not have the same level of flexibility as motorized beds. They are typically used in settings where advanced positioning features are not required, such as post-operative recovery rooms or general patient rooms.</p></p>
